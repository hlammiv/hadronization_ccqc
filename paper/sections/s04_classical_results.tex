\begin{figure}[t]

\caption{Static diquark-formation probability

\subsection{Species formation in the expanding plasma}

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig5_scan_H0.pdf}
\caption{Species yields per event versus expansion rate $H_0$
(24 events/point, $N=604$, bootstrap errors).  Faster quenches capture
fewer mesons but a slightly larger baryon fraction; no pentaquark
occurred in these 72 events ($<0.125$/event at 95\%~CL).}
\label{fig:scanH0}
\end{figure}

Across $H_0 = 0.05$--$0.20\,c/\fm$ the meson yield falls from
$40.0\pm1.1$ to $35.0\pm0.9$ per event while (anti)baryons rise from
$\sim0.46$ to $\sim0.69$ per species (Fig.~\ref{fig:scanH0});
tetraquarks appear at the few-percent-per-event level.

\subsection{Constituent-number penalty}

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig6_penalty.pdf}
\caption{Yield per event versus constituent number at two couplings;
bars with arrows are 95\%~CL Poisson upper limits (no pentaquark
observed at either point).  The penalty per added constituent is
$\sim60\times$ ($2\to3$) and $\sim15\times$ ($3\to4$) at
$\Gamma\approx0.8$, flattening to $\sim 70\times$ and $\sim8\times$ at
$\Gamma\approx1.2$.}
\label{fig:penalty}
\end{figure}

The yields fall steeply and approximately geometrically with constituent
count --- $63\times$ from meson to baryon and $15\times$ from baryon to
tetraquark at default coupling --- with the $3\to4$ step flattening to
$8\times$ at the stronger-coupling corner (Fig.~\ref{fig:penalty}).
This is the qualitative structure coalescence models predict
\cite{Fries:2008hs,ExHIC:2010gcb}; the slope and its $\Gamma$-dependence
are this model's quantitative output.  Four pentaquark clusters occurred
in the $\tau_{\rm bound}$ scan, but all descend from the same
equilibrated initial configuration (one seed recurring across scan
points), so they constitute a single independent formation event ---
production-scale statistics or biased seeding are required, as is a
larger charm program: no manifestly flavor-exotic ($cc\bar q\bar q$)
tetraquark occurred in 180 events with 2--4 injected $c\bar c$ pairs.

\subsection{Annihilation timescales and flavor chemistry}

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig7_scan_tau.pdf}
\caption{Species yields versus bound-pair lifetime $\tau_{\rm bound}$ at
pure photon branching ($P_\gamma=1$).  Meson depletion is mild because
the bound-pair duty cycle inside $d_{\rm ann}$ is small (effective
lifetimes $\gg$ the simulated window); the apparent (anti)baryon
enhancement at finite $\tau_{\rm bound}$ is a $\sim2\sigma$-per-channel
effect requiring production statistics.}
\label{fig:scantau}
\end{figure}

At $P_\gamma=1$ every annihilation removes a pair permanently: $84\pm3$
photons escape per event, dominated by hot-phase scattering annihilation
rather than bound-state decay (Fig.~\ref{fig:scantau}).  The planned
lifetime-calibration observable (measured decay rate of prepared bound
mesons versus $\tau_{\rm bound}$) will convert the duty-cycle factor
into an effective meson width.  \todo{strangeness fraction vs $t$;

\subsection{The diquark question, dynamically}

Across every scan point --- expansion rates spanning a factor of four,
couplings up to $\Gamma\approx1.2$, and all annihilation settings --- the
diquark-first fraction is
\begin{equation}
f_{\rm diquark\text{-}first} = 0.86\text{--}1.00,
\end{equation}
with most points consistent with unity (e.g.\ $1.00$ at $H_0=0.10$,
$0.96\pm0.04$ at $0.05$, $0.86\pm0.07$ at the corner).  Dynamical
evolution \emph{amplifies} the static nearest-neighbor probability of
Ref.~\cite{Lebed:2016jvq}: once a diquark binds, it survives to seed a
baryon.  \todo{Fig.~9: $f$ vs $\Gamma$, $H_0$ with the static band;
