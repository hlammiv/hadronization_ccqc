\subsection{Static validation and the correlated gas}

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig2_static.pdf}
\caption{Static diquark-formation probability
[Eq.~\eqref{eq:pstatic}, hard wall, $k=\sqrt2$] versus per-species
density: Lebed's counting ($n_1{=}3n_2$) and the discrete-color counting
($n_1{=}2n_2$), analytic curves with ideal-gas Monte Carlo points; crosses
are Table~2 of Ref.~\cite{Lebed:2016jvq}.  The countings coincide in the
dilute (purely geometric) limit, cross at intermediate density, and
saturate at $51.5\%$ vs $41.4\%$.}
\label{fig:static}
\end{figure}

Our Lebed-mode Monte Carlo reproduces the published hard-wall value at
reference density, $50.45\pm0.12\%$ vs $50.29\%$, and
Fig.~\ref{fig:static} extends the comparison across five decades of
density in both counting modes.

Beyond the ideal gas, we apply the same estimator to the
\emph{interacting} equilibrated configurations at the simulation's own
density ($n_0R^3=1$, $\Gamma\approx0.8$).  The paired
(equilibrated$-$ideal, same configurations) shift is
$+0.028\pm0.035$ (Lebed counting) and $+0.016\pm0.029$ (discrete
counting): interaction correlations move the static probability by at
most a few percentage points at this coupling, with a positive central
value.  \todo{production statistics for a definitive sign; $k$-dependence
panel.}

\subsection{Constituent-number penalty}

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig6_penalty.pdf}
\caption{Yield per event versus constituent number at two couplings;
bars with arrows are 95\%~CL Poisson upper limits (no pentaquark
observed at either point).  The penalty per added constituent is
$\sim60\times$ ($2\to3$) and $\sim15\times$ ($3\to4$) at
$\Gamma\approx0.8$, flattening to $\sim 70\times$ and $\sim8\times$ at
$\Gamma\approx1.2$.}
\label{fig:penalty}
\end{figure}

The yields fall steeply and approximately geometrically with constituent
count --- $63\times$ from meson to baryon and $15\times$ from baryon to
tetraquark at default coupling --- with the $3\to4$ step flattening to
$8\times$ at the stronger-coupling corner (Fig.~\ref{fig:penalty}).
This is the qualitative structure coalescence models predict
\cite{Fries:2008hs,ExHIC:2010gcb}; the slope and its $\Gamma$-dependence
are this model's quantitative output.  Four pentaquark clusters occurred
in the $\tau_{\rm bound}$ scan, but all descend from the same
equilibrated initial configuration (one seed recurring across scan
points), so they constitute a single independent formation event ---
production-scale statistics or biased seeding are required, as is a
larger charm program: no manifestly flavor-exotic ($cc\bar q\bar q$)
tetraquark occurred in 180 events with 2--4 injected $c\bar c$ pairs.

\subsection{Annihilation timescales and flavor chemistry}

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig7_scan_tau.pdf}
\caption{Species yields versus bound-pair lifetime $\tau_{\rm bound}$ at
pure photon branching ($P_\gamma=1$).  Meson depletion is mild because
the bound-pair duty cycle inside $d_{\rm ann}$ is small (effective
lifetimes $\gg$ the simulated window); the apparent (anti)baryon
enhancement at finite $\tau_{\rm bound}$ is a $\sim2\sigma$-per-channel
effect requiring production statistics.}
\label{fig:scantau}
\end{figure}

At $P_\gamma=1$ every annihilation removes a pair permanently: $84\pm3$
photons escape per event, dominated by hot-phase scattering annihilation
rather than bound-state decay (Fig.~\ref{fig:scantau}).  The
lifetime calibration makes this quantitative: prepared bound mesons with
a reference orbit (duty cycle $0.386$ inside $d_{\rm ann}$) decay with
$\tau_{\rm eff} = (7.2\text{--}7.3)\,\tau_{\rm bound}$, measured across a
factor of four in $\tau_{\rm bound}$ and consistent with the predicted
$3/\text{duty} = 7.8$; wider orbits have smaller duty cycles and
correspondingly longer effective lifetimes, which is why late-forming
mesons deplete only mildly within the simulated window.

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig8_chemistry.pdf}
\caption{Chemistry-active slow-quench event ($H_0=0.02\,c/\fm$,
$\lambda_{\rm scatt}=20\,c/\fm$, $P_\gamma=0.3$,
$\tau_{\rm bound}=10\fm/c$): (a) net strangeness production through
thermal-weight re-injection (the classical strangeness-enhancement
analog), $N_s/N$ rising from $0.232$ to $0.251$; (b) escaped-photon pair
energies, peaked near $2m_q$ plus thermal motion.}
\label{fig:chem}
\end{figure}

With the gluon channel active, flavor chemistry operates:
Fig.~\ref{fig:chem} shows a slow-quench event in which re-injection at
falling $\Teff$ raises the strange fraction by two percentage points
while 55 annihilations escape to the photon ledger.

\subsection{The diquark question, dynamically}

Across every scan point --- expansion rates spanning a factor of four,
couplings up to $\Gamma\approx1.2$, and all annihilation settings --- the
diquark-first fraction is
\begin{equation}
f_{\rm diquark\text{-}first} = 0.86\text{--}1.00,
\end{equation}
with most points consistent with unity (e.g.\ $1.00$ at $H_0=0.10$,
$0.96\pm0.04$ at $0.05$, $0.86\pm0.07$ at the corner;
Fig.~\ref{fig:dqfirst}).  Dynamical evolution \emph{amplifies} the
static nearest-neighbor probability of Ref.~\cite{Lebed:2016jvq}: once
a diquark binds, it survives to seed a baryon.

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig9_dqfirst.pdf}
\caption{Diquark-first fraction of baryons versus expansion rate at two
couplings, compared with the static nearest-neighbor band evaluated at
the simulation density ($n_0R^3=1$; band spans the Lebed and
discrete-color countings).  Dynamics amplifies the static probability
toward unity.}
\label{fig:dqfirst}
\end{figure}
