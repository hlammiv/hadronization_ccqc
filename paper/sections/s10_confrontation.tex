\section{Confrontation: classical cartoon, quantum framework, phenomenology}
\label{sec:confrontation}

Three independent handles now aim at the same ratios:

\begin{enumerate}
\item the classical simulation of Secs.~\ref{sec:model}--%
\ref{sec:classical-results} (cheap, dynamical, schematic color);
\item the Tier-1--4 lattice observables of Sec.~\ref{sec:hierarchy}

\item phenomenological baselines: statistical hadronization at

\subsection{Beyond heavy ions: pp and \texorpdfstring{$p\bar p$}{ppbar}}

Nothing in either half is intrinsically heavy-ion.  The classical model
assumes only a deconfined quark ensemble that expands and hadronizes;
for minimum-bias pp that assumption competes with vacuum fragmentation,
but the small-systems phenomenology (continuity of strangeness and
baryon ratios in multiplicity across pp, $p$Pb, and PbPb) indicates
medium-like hadronization turns on already in high-multiplicity pp.
Three structural points favor the small-system application.  First, the
model is \emph{canonical by construction}: color, flavor, and baryon
number are conserved exactly event by event --- precisely the
correction grand-canonical statistical hadronization needs in small
systems.  Second, our simulated sizes ($N=10^2$--$3\times10^3$ quarks)
span high-multiplicity pp through peripheral AA.  Third, midrapidity at
collider energies is net-baryon-free for pp, $p\bar p$, and AA alike,
matching the $B=0$ initialization, so Tevatron prompt-$\chic$
production data are legitimate anchors alongside LHC pp.

\begin{figure}[t]
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figs/fig10_smallsys.pdf}
\caption{Yield ratios to mesons versus system size at fixed density,
temperature, and $H_0=0.1\,c/\fm$.  Baryon-to-meson is flat from
$N=300$ to $N=3000$ ($1.40\pm0.36\%$ vs $1.46\pm0.10\%$) but drops to
$0.46\pm0.46\%$ at $N=96$ --- a canonical-style suppression of
composite species in the smallest droplets ($2\sigma$ with current
statistics); no tetraquark occurred at $N=96$.}
\label{fig:smallsys}
\end{figure}

A first size scan (Fig.~\ref{fig:smallsys}) shows the expected
canonical trend: composite-to-meson ratios are size-independent above
$N\approx300$ and suppressed in the smallest droplets --- the classical
analog of the steepening light-nuclei penalty from PbPb to pp and a
prediction for how exotic-to-charmonium ratios should scale with event
multiplicity, the observable LHCb has begun to measure for
$\chic$.

The crosswalks are explicit by construction.  The classical
manifest-exotic tally ($cc\bar u\bar d$ versus $c\bar c q\bar q$) is the
same partition Tier~1 draws with conserved charges.  The classical binding
criterion maps onto Tier-4 flowed projectors, with
$r_0 \leftrightarrow \sqrt{8t_{\rm fl}}$ as the scheme dial; comparing the
$\tau_{\rm bound}$-scanned classical ratios with $t_f$-scanned Tier-1--3
counts separates formation from survival in both frameworks.
